# Research Note: MVP Buildout Scope, Must-Haves, and Timeline

## Bottom line

There are two honest paths:

- a **lean pilot rig** to prove mechanics and customer behavior
- a **public-facing flagship MVP** that is safe, branded, and commercially credible

Trying to talk about both as if they cost the same is what keeps the build discussion fuzzy.

## Must-haves for any playable version

- 3 to 4 hitting bays or hitting positions
- clear 111-yard shot geometry
- commercial-grade safety netting or equivalent containment
- one illuminated stock-tank water target
- one floating green target with flag
- ball-dispensing workflow
- target verification and camera coverage
- basic lighting, networking, and electrical

## What can stay lean initially

- the target can be a simple galvanized stock tank, not a custom pool
- the first retrieval workflow can be simpler than a full engineered vacuum system
- a private pilot can use interim tracking hardware before final commercial hardware is locked
- the first structure can be more modular than polished

## Hardware and cost anchors

### Tracking

- FlightScope announced `Range Gen2` in January 2026 as a commercial outdoor and multi-bay product at **$1,699** each, with launch targeted for **June/July 2026**.
- FlightScope `Mevo+` is publicly listed at **$1,099**, but it is better viewed as a consumer/prototype tool rather than the final commercial install answer.

### Dispensing

- JFM Golf lists commercial electric ball dispensers at **$7,295-$7,695**.
- Range Servant's high-capacity dispensers are quote-based, which suggests a commercial final stack may move above entry-level public pricing.

### Ball handling

- Hollrock's Jan. 2025 quick guide lists ball washers from **$2,995-$4,995** and water-jet pre-wash/sorter hardware at **$5,095**.
- A true MVP may not need full washer/elevator automation on day one if operations stay light.

### Target

- Tractor Supply lists a Behlen Country 10-foot galvanized tank at **$799.99**.
- Expect additional cost for sealing, support, lighting, float fabrication, and water management.

### Cameras

- Ubiquiti lists the `G5 Bullet` at **$129** and the `UNVR` at **$299**.
- That makes a basic multi-camera verification stack relatively affordable compared with netting and civil work.

### Netting

Netting is likely the hidden budget killer.

- A 2024 municipal golf study snippet priced range netting at **$2.50 per square foot**, with **90,000 square feet = $225,000**.
- That same study snippet priced **200 feet of concrete hitting bays with mats at $50,000**.

These are not direct apples-to-apples numbers for Lucas, but they reinforce that permanent safety containment can dominate the build budget quickly.

## Practical cost posture

### Lean pilot / proof rig

Likely range: **$40k-$90k**

Best use:

- validate the shot
- test pricing
- prove scoring and video
- learn what the flagship really needs

### Public-facing flagship MVP

Likely range: **$150k-$350k+**

Best use:

- branded first site
- customer-facing experience
- camera-ready flagship
- repeatable template for future rollout

## Timeline

### Lean private pilot

Expected range: **4-8 weeks**

### Public-facing flagship MVP

Expected range: **8-16+ weeks**

The physical assembly may be quick once materials are in hand. Site prep, electrical, data, netting fabrication, procurement, and approvals are the real schedule drivers.

## Strategic implication

The right near-term move is probably:

1. lock the flagship thesis
2. price the pilot honestly
3. prove the target, cameras, and shot format
4. then decide how polished the Lucas flagship should be

That keeps momentum without pretending the first commercial build is a two-day install.
