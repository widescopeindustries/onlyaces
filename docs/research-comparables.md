# Research Note: Stateside Comparables and Blind Spots

## Bottom line

The U.S. market is not empty. It is fragmented and still mostly water-dependent.

That is useful because it means the first-mover claim needs to be precise:

- not "nobody in America has tried a floating-green prize shot"
- but "nobody appears to have clearly scaled a land-based flagship version that removes the lake requirement"

## Comparable 1: Sink or Win

Current posture:

- Markets itself as a high-stakes hole-in-one challenge
- Says it is "now launching across DFW"
- Uses a floating green and course/venue partnerships
- Prices include **$5 for one shot**, **5 shots for $20**, **10 shots for $35**

Read:

- Strong evidence of Texas demand and concept viability
- Still dependent on water and partner venues
- Appears more pop-up / course-network than standalone branded flagship

Source: https://www.sinkorwin.com/

## Comparable 2: TheMoneyShot.golf

Current posture:

- Markets a **$10,000** floating-island challenge
- Says it is a "fully automated" experience
- Prices include **10 balls for $20** and **25 balls for $40**
- Builds the story around shareable 360-degree video and a leaderboard

Read:

- Validates the automation-plus-content angle
- Still framed around floating island greens in water hazards
- Appears venue-dependent rather than portable to ordinary land parcels

Source: https://themoneyshot.golf/index2

## Comparable 3: Chip 2 Win

Current posture:

- Markets a mobile **$10,000** hole-in-one challenge
- Explicitly pitches tournaments, corporate events, parks, marinas, and permanent venue partnerships
- Says hosts need a scenic body of water or existing water feature

Read:

- Confirms U.S. appetite for prize-shot concepts
- Strongly suggests water remains the assumed requirement
- Business looks like a service/event model more than a fixed-site flagship model

Source: https://chip2win.com/

## Comparable 4: Club and resort installations

Examples such as Bartram Trail Golf Club and Laughlin Ranch show floating-green challenges can work as side attractions or event features.

Read:

- They validate the attraction value
- They do not appear to validate a scaled, standalone branded business template

Sources:

- https://bartramtrailgolfclub.org/hole-in-one-floating-green-challenge/
- https://laughlinranch.com/golf/hole-in-one-challenge-2025/

## Strategic read

Three patterns keep showing up:

1. **Water dependency**
   - Existing concepts still assume a pond, lake, cove, or water feature.

2. **Pop-up or venue-partner structure**
   - The business often travels to events or attaches to an existing golf/resort asset.

3. **No clear land-based flagship template**
   - I did not find strong evidence of a scaled, land-based U.S. flagship that proves the model without natural water.

## Why this helps Only Aces

It means the real market opportunity may be:

- not inventing the prize-shot category
- but removing the last structural bottleneck keeping it niche

That is a better and more defensible first-mover claim.
