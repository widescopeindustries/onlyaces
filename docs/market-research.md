# Market Research Notes

## North Texas demand case

- The National Golf Foundation says **28.1 million Americans played on-course golf in 2024**, the highest since 2008.
  Source: https://www.ngf.org/short-game/golfs-state-of-industry-in-3-minutes/
- Texas' official golf-impact project says Texas has **1.89 million individual on-course golfers**, equal to **6.29% of the state population**, ranking **second nationally** for on-course and total participation.
  Source: https://www.texasgolfimpact.org/abouttexasgolfimpact
- The City of Frisco says the PGA of America's headquarters moved to Frisco and the campus opened in **August 2022** as the **Modern Home of American Golf**.
  Source: https://www.friscotexas.gov/1487/PGA-of-America
- D Magazine reported in **April 2024** that North Texas is home to nearly **40 professional golfers**, including roughly **20 PGA Tour pros**, plus LPGA and LIV players.
  Source: https://www.dmagazine.com/publications/d-magazine/2024/april/meet-some-of-north-texass-pro-swingers/
- U.S. Census QuickFacts lists **Collin County median household income at $121,600** in 2024 dollars for **2020-2024**, with **56.2%** of adults holding a bachelor's degree or higher.
  Source: https://www.census.gov/quickfacts/fact/table/collincountytexas/PST045225

## Safe takeaways

- Texas is already one of the strongest golf states in America by participation.
- North Texas now has one of the densest golf ecosystems in the country, anchored by PGA Frisco and a deep bench of pros and facilities.
- Collin County adds the affluent consumer base needed for a premium impulse entertainment concept.
