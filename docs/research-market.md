# Research Note: Market, Demographics, and Corridor Fit

## National golf demand

Recent NGF data supports the broad demand thesis.

- NGF said total U.S. golf participation hit **47.2 million in 2024**.
- NGF said green-grass participation topped **28.1 million in 2024**.
- NGF said total golf participation climbed to **more than 48 million in 2025**, while green-grass participation surpassed **29 million**.

This matters because Only Aces depends on golf interest being broad enough to support a premium challenge concept, not just traditional tee-time play.

## Texas golf posture

Texas is a legitimate golf state, even using NGF's older state snapshot.

- NGF's Texas one-pager lists **715 total facilities**.
- The same sheet implies roughly **1.74 million golfers** statewide in 2020 based on male and female totals.
- It also shows a strong index for juniors and non-white golfers, suggesting breadth beyond a narrow legacy base.

## North Texas corridor fit

North Texas is the part that makes the flagship argument stronger.

- The City of Frisco says the PGA of America opened its Frisco headquarters in **August 2022**, with championship golf, a retail village, and the Omni PGA Frisco resort around it.
- D Magazine reported in **April 2024** that North Texas is home to **almost 40 professional golfers**, including around **20 PGA Tour pros** in and around Dallas.
- North Texas also benefits from year-round practice conditions relative to colder golf regions.

## Lucas demographic fit

Lucas looks attractive for premium impulse entertainment:

- Median household income: **$221,364**
- Median owner-occupied home value: **$941,900**
- Bachelor's degree or higher: **65.1%**
- Owner-occupied housing rate: **92.3%**
- Population growth from 2020 base to 2024 estimate: **16.3%**

The takeaway is not simply that Lucas is wealthy. It is that Lucas sits in a fast-growing, high-discretionary-spend corridor where a premium challenge format makes more sense than a commodity-price range model.

## Implication for Only Aces

The flagship thesis is stronger if it is framed as:

- premium impulse recreation
- golf-adjacent but not golf-exclusive
- content-capable
- fast to understand
- built for an affluent suburban corridor rather than a destination resort
